    Unai Eibar Arambarri

    After six enriching years at the engineering school of Bilbao, where I specialized in mechanical engineering, I pursued a brief but intense French stage in which I was able to study and work in the fields of robotics and circular economy. Currently, I am working towards my PhD in applied engineering at Ceit-BRTA. As part of my research, I am training in fracture mechanics and working on developing and combining finite elements models and analytical models for crack propagation on round rebars in reinforced concrete.

    Projects

    Smart Disassembly For Circularity (SDC2)

    In SDC2, a multi-technology human-robot automatic cell was developed to test and define some potential disassembly lines to face products such as electric motors, power converters, big electric wastes (WEEE), and later battery, H2 PAC or other products related to the Extended Responsibilities of Producers (sports and leisure, gardening …). The platform development involved 6 French laboratories and industrial partners and started at the end of 2022.

    SUREWAVE

    The main objective of this European project is to develop and test an innovative concept of a Floating Photo-Voltaic (FPV) system consisting of an external floating breakwater structure made of new circular materials acting as protection against severe wave loads on the FPV structure itself, allowing increased operational availability and energy output. Such a concept is able to operate in all the European sea basins including open-sea very harsh environments with high wind (speed >25 m/s), current (>1.2 m/s), and wave (height >14 m), thus unlocking the massive deployment of Offshore FPV at European and worldwide level.
